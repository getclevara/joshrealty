The Big Trade-Off

No state income tax. If you earn $150,000, you're saving $7,000-12,000/year compared to states like New York, California, or New Jersey. For high earners, the tax savings alone can offset higher housing and insurance costs. Use our True Cost Calculator to run your specific numbers.

Groceries and Dining

Groceries run about 5-10% above the national average. Publix is the dominant grocery chain and is reliable but not the cheapest. Costco, Aldi, and Sedano's (Latin grocery) offer better value. Dining out is competitive - Miami's restaurant scene is world-class with options at every price point. Expect $15-20 per person for casual dining, $40-80 for mid-range, and $100+ for fine dining.

Transportation

You need a car. Public transit exists (Metrorail in Miami, Tri-Rail between counties) but is not a substitute for driving. Average commute: 30-45 minutes. Gas prices typically match or slightly exceed the national average. Car insurance in Florida averages $2,500-3,500/year - the third highest in the nation. The Brightline train connects Miami, Fort Lauderdale, and West Palm Beach and is genuinely useful for that corridor.

Honest Advice

Visit before you commit. Rent for 3-6 months if you can. Every neighborhood has a completely different feel, and "South Florida" is not one place - it's three counties spanning 100+ miles. What works for your family depends on your commute, school needs, cultural preferences, and lifestyle. That's exactly what we help people figure out.

Taxes, Insurance, and the Fine Print

The financial stuff that surprises most people moving to Florida.

The Tax Picture

No state income tax. This is real and it's significant. But Florida makes up revenue in other ways.

File for Homestead Exemption

If this is your primary residence, file for Homestead Exemption by March 1 of the year after you buy. It saves up to $50,000 off your assessed value AND caps assessment increases at 3% per year (Save Our Homes amendment). This is free. Don't miss it. File at your county Property Appraiser's office.

Get insurance quotes BEFORE you close on a home. Insurance availability and cost vary dramatically by location, construction type, and roof age. A beautiful home with a 15-year-old roof may be nearly uninsurable at a reasonable cost. We help our clients factor this in from day one.

Schools: What Families Need to Know

South Florida has excellent public and private school options, but quality varies significantly by area.

Public School Districts

Miami-Dade County Public Schools is the 4th largest district in the US. Overall district grade: B. But individual school quality ranges dramatically. The magnet and school choice programs are strong - you're not limited to your zoned school.

Broward County Public Schools is the 6th largest. Some of the state's top-performing schools are here (Weston, Parkland, Coral Springs). The district offers robust magnet and charter options.

Palm Beach County School District is consistently rated among the top large districts in Florida. Strong schools in Boca Raton, Jupiter, and Wellington.

School Choice in Florida

Florida has robust school choice. You can apply to magnet schools, charter schools, and inter-district transfer programs. You're not locked into your zoned school. Research options at your county's school choice office before you buy. Sometimes a more affordable neighborhood + school choice = better outcome than overpaying for a "school zone" address.

The Stuff Nobody Puts in the Brochure

After helping 1,200+ families move to South Florida, here's what we wish someone had told them upfront.

The Heat Is Real

May through October is hot and humid. Not "oh it's a warm day" hot - genuinely oppressive if you're not used to it. Your AC will run almost continuously for 6 months. Your electric bill will reflect that ($200-350/month in summer). You'll plan outdoor activities for early morning or evening. You'll get used to it, but it takes a full year.

Traffic Is Part of the Deal

I-95, the Palmetto, 826, the Turnpike - they're all congested during rush hours. A 15-mile commute can take 45 minutes. This is not a "it's getting worse" situation - it has been bad for decades. Factor commute into your neighborhood decision. Living close to work is worth a higher price per square foot.

Spanish Is Everywhere (and That's a Feature)

Miami-Dade is 70%+ Hispanic. Many businesses operate primarily in Spanish. You don't need Spanish to live here, but knowing even basic conversational Spanish enriches your experience enormously. Broward and Palm Beach are more English-dominant but increasingly multilingual. The cultural diversity is genuine and one of the best things about living here.

Hurricanes: Respect, Don't Fear

Yes, hurricanes are real. No, they don't hit every year. South Florida's building codes since Hurricane Andrew (1992) are among the strictest in the nation. Modern construction with impact windows handles most storms well. You prepare, you have a plan, you stay aware during season, and most years nothing happens. The people who struggle are the ones who don't prepare at all or who panic when a storm forms. Check out our Hurricane Readiness Kit for details.

The Social Scene Takes Effort

South Florida is transient. People come and go. Building a social circle takes intentional effort - join a church, gym, or community group. Kids make it easier (school parent networks are strong). The good news: everyone here came from somewhere else, so people are generally open and welcoming. Nobody will judge you for being "new."

It's Worth It

For all the caveats, people who move here and commit to it overwhelmingly love it. The weather (9 months of the year), the beaches, the cultural diversity, the food, the energy, the no-income-tax, the outdoor lifestyle, the proximity to the Caribbean and Latin America - there's a reason 1,000+ people move to Florida every day. The key is going in with clear eyes about the trade-offs.
